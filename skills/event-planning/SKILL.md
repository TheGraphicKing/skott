## Overview
This skill manages end-to-end event planning for Lyzr-hosted or Lyzr-sponsored events — from webinars and virtual summits to in-person field events and conference sponsorships. It produces a structured plan covering every phase: pre-event production, promotion, day-of execution, and post-event follow-up.

## When to Use
- Planning a webinar, virtual event, or roundtable
- Managing a conference sponsorship or booth
- Organizing a customer advisory board (CAB) or executive dinner
- Launching a live demo day or product launch event
- As the first step in the event-launch workflow

## How to Execute

### Step 1: Define Event Parameters
Establish the foundational decisions:
- **Event type**: Webinar, virtual summit, in-person conference, roundtable, field event, partner event
- **Goal**: Pipeline generation, brand awareness, customer expansion, partner enablement
- **Target audience**: Persona, company size, geography, seniority
- **Expected attendance**: Registrations vs. attendees (typical show rate: 40-60% for webinars)
- **Date, time, and platform**: Check for competitive events and time zone coverage
- **Budget**: Speaker fees, platform costs, promotion budget, production costs

### Step 2: Build the Agenda
For a 60-minute webinar format (most common):
- 0-5 min: Welcome, housekeeping, speaker intros
- 5-15 min: Problem framing / industry context (speaker 1)
- 15-35 min: Main content / presentation (speaker 2 or panel)
- 35-50 min: Live demo or case study (Lyzr presenter)
- 50-60 min: Q&A

For in-person events, build a full session schedule with:
- Session titles, descriptions, and lengths
- Breaks and networking time
- Keynote vs. breakout vs. workshop designations
- AV and logistics requirements per session

### Step 3: Speaker Management Plan

**Speaker recruitment**:
- Identify 2-3 target speakers per event: mix of Lyzr executives, customers, and industry thought leaders
- Outreach sequence: initial invitation → follow-up → confirmation → briefing → rehearsal
- Provide speaker deck template and brand guidelines

**Speaker timeline**:
- T-8 weeks: Speaker confirmed
- T-6 weeks: Speaker brief sent (topic, audience, format, expected outcome)
- T-4 weeks: Draft slides due
- T-2 weeks: Rehearsal scheduled
- T-1 week: Final slides due
- T-1 day: Tech check and final confirmation

### Step 4: Promotion Timeline
Run promotion in three phases:

**Phase 1: Announcement (T-4 to T-3 weeks)**:
- Event landing page live with registration form
- Email announcement to full database
- LinkedIn organic post (brand + key speakers)
- Paid LinkedIn campaign (if budgeted)
- Speaker promotion pack sent to speakers for their own channels

**Phase 2: Nurture (T-3 to T-1 week)**:
- Email reminder to non-registrants from announcement
- LinkedIn retargeting to website visitors
- Blog post or thought leadership piece tied to the event topic
- Speaker social posts (coordinate timing)
- Partner promotion (if applicable)

**Phase 3: Last Push (T-7 to T-1 days)**:
- "Last chance" email to engaged non-registrants
- Day-before reminder email to all registrants
- Social posts with "tomorrow" urgency
- Paid boost on top-performing organic posts

### Step 5: Registration Tracking
Set up tracking before registration opens:
- UTM parameters for every promotional link (source/medium/campaign)
- Weekly registration report: total registrations, source breakdown, pace vs. target
- Registration target: set 2x target (accounting for ~50% show rate)
- Segment registrants by persona and company size — this feeds the lead-capture and post-event-nurture skills

### Step 6: Day-Of Execution Checklist
- Tech check 30-60 minutes before start
- Moderator briefed and on standby
- Chat moderation assigned (separate from presenter)
- Recording enabled
- Q&A queue monitored live
- Attendance and engagement metrics captured

### Step 7: Post-Event Follow-Up (see post-event-nurture for full detail)
Immediately after:
- Export attendee and no-show lists with engagement data
- Upload to CRM with event tag and engagement score
- Trigger post-event nurture sequence within 24 hours

## Overview
This skill produces original long-form B2B SaaS content that combines search engine optimization with genuine editorial quality. The output reads like it was written by a practitioner, not generated by a template. Every piece has a clear argument, structured narrative, and calls to action aligned to the buyer journey stage.

## When to Use
- Creating new content to target keyword clusters from keyword-research
- Building thought leadership assets (CMO bylines, industry perspectives)
- Developing gated assets (playbooks, frameworks, guides) for lead generation
- Writing customer-facing case studies and success stories
- Producing campaign-specific landing page content

## How to Execute

### Step 1: Define the Brief
Before writing, confirm:
- Content type: blog post, case study, playbook, ebook chapter, landing page body
- Primary keyword and 4-6 secondary keywords
- Target persona and funnel stage (awareness / consideration / decision)
- Angle/unique POV: what is the ONE thing this piece argues or proves that others don't?
- Word count target: Blog (1,200-2,500), Pillar (3,000-5,000), Playbook (5,000-10,000+)
- Desired CTA: demo request, content download, newsletter signup, etc.
- Brand voice requirements (see brand-guidelines.md)

### Step 2: SERP and Competitor Analysis
Before writing:
- Review top 5 results for the primary keyword
- Identify what every top result covers (the table stakes)
- Identify what none of them cover (the differentiation opportunity)
- Note the angle, tone, and format of the top-ranking piece
- Plan to be more comprehensive, more specific, or more opinionated than the competition

### Step 3: Outline
Structure the outline before writing the content. A strong B2B SaaS outline includes:

For a **blog post**:
1. Hook — Counterintuitive stat, bold claim, or vivid scenario
2. The problem — Articulate the reader's pain with specificity
3. Why current solutions fail — Earn trust by acknowledging complexity
4. The framework/solution — Original POV or structured approach
5. Deep-dive sections (H2s) — One per major aspect of the solution
6. Evidence — Data points, quotes, examples woven throughout
7. Objection handling — Address the "yes but..." moments
8. Conclusion with forward-looking insight
9. CTA — Specific next step, not generic "contact us"

For a **case study**:
1. Customer headline — Specific result, not vague praise
2. Customer profile — Who they are, context
3. The challenge — What they were struggling with before
4. Why they chose Lyzr — The decision moment
5. The solution — What was implemented and how
6. The results — Quantified outcomes (revenue, time saved, efficiency gains)
7. Quote from champion — In their own words
8. Next steps / expansion — Shows ongoing value

For a **playbook**:
1. Executive summary (2-3 paragraphs)
2. The problem this playbook solves
3. Prerequisites and assumptions
4. Framework overview (visual if possible)
5. Step-by-step sections with examples
6. Common mistakes and how to avoid them
7. Measurement and success criteria
8. Resources and next steps

### Step 4: Write to the Outline
Craft the content following these principles:
- **Specific over vague**: "43% of CMOs report X" beats "many CMOs report X"
- **Show, don't tell**: Use examples, mini-scenarios, and analogies
- **Active voice**: "Marketing teams waste 6 hours per week on manual reporting" beats "6 hours per week are wasted"
- **Short sentences in high-impact moments**: Build rhythm. Short. Then a longer sentence to carry the idea forward.
- **No Lyzr brand voice violations**: see brand-guidelines.md — never say "easy" or "simple"
- **Earn every paragraph**: Each paragraph must add something new. Cut anything that repeats.

### Step 5: Weave in SEO
- Primary keyword in: H1, first 100 words, at least 2 H2s, meta description
- Secondary keywords distributed naturally throughout
- Internal links to 3-5 related Lyzr content pieces (suggest relevant targets)
- External links to 2-3 authoritative sources (adds E-E-A-T credibility)

### Step 6: CTAs
Place CTAs:
- Once in the first third (for scanners who don't read to the end)
- Once after your strongest proof point
- Once at the end
Each CTA should be specific to what just happened in the narrative — not a generic banner.

- Meta description: [140-160 chars]
- Primary keyword: [keyword]
- Word count: [count]
- Target persona: [persona name]
- Funnel stage: [Awareness / Consideration / Decision]
- CTA: [what you're asking readers to do]

**Full Content**: [Complete, publish-ready piece with proper H1/H2/H3 formatting]

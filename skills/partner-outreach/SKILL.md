## Overview
This skill builds and executes a structured partner outreach program. It covers partner identification, fit scoring, outreach sequencing, and pipeline tracking. The output is a prioritized partner shortlist with tailored outreach templates ready to deploy — focused on relationships that can generate mutual pipeline, not just logo collections.

## When to Use
- Launching a new partner or channel program
- Expanding co-marketing or integration partnerships
- When organic and paid channels need supplementing with referral/partner pipeline
- When a specific market or vertical is best reached through a trusted third party
- Before building co-marketing campaigns (feeds into co-marketing skill)

## How to Execute

### Step 1: Define Partnership Goals
Before researching partners, clarify:
- **Partnership type**: Technology integration, channel/reseller, co-marketing, agency, industry association
- **Primary goal**: Pipeline generation, brand credibility, market access, product distribution
- **Success metric**: Number of referrals, co-branded leads, joint pipeline $ per quarter
- **Resources available**: Dedicated partner manager? What co-marketing budget exists?

### Step 2: Build the Partner Criteria Framework
Score potential partners against these dimensions:

**Audience Fit (1-5)**: Does their audience match Lyzr's ICP? Are they reaching CMOs and marketing leaders at mid-market to enterprise B2B companies?

**Complementary Positioning (1-5)**: Do they solve an adjacent problem without competing directly? (e.g., a marketing data analytics tool, an agency, a CRM consultancy, a content distribution platform)

**Audience Size and Engagement (1-5)**: Do they have an active audience — newsletter subscribers, LinkedIn following, event attendees — that trusts their recommendations?

**Organizational Fit (1-5)**: Are they roughly similar in go-to-market maturity? Avoid massive enterprise partners (you'll get deprioritized) and micro-players (limited reach).

**Reciprocity Potential (1-5)**: Is there something meaningful Lyzr can offer them in return? (Co-marketing budget, product integration, audience access, content contribution)

Minimum partnership score: 15/25 to pursue actively.

### Step 3: Research and Build the Longlist
Identify 30-50 potential partners from:
- **Technology ecosystem**: Companies that integrate with tools in your customers' stack (HubSpot, Salesforce, Marketo, Google Analytics, Slack)
- **Analyst and media**: Marketing-focused publications, newsletters, and analysts (e.g., ChiefMartec, The Hustle, Demand Gen Report)
- **Agencies**: Marketing transformation and RevOps agencies that implement tools for enterprise clients
- **Events**: Co-sponsors and speakers from events your ICP attends
- **LinkedIn**: Look at who your best customers follow and engage with
- **Competitor partner pages**: Which companies partner with Jasper, Writer, or HubSpot? Some may be winnable.

### Step 4: Score and Shortlist
Apply the scoring framework to each candidate. Focus active outreach on:
- Top 10: Score 20+ — prioritize this month
- Next 15: Score 15-19 — pursue over next quarter
- Remaining: Hold for later or monitor

### Step 5: Research Each Priority Partner
Before outreaching to any partner, know:
- Their company's go-to-market motion (inbound vs. outbound, PLG vs. sales-led)
- Recent content, campaigns, or partnerships they've announced
- Who the right contact is (Head of Partnerships, CMO, co-founder)
- Any shared customers or mutual connections that can warm the intro

### Step 6: Draft Outreach Sequence

**Email 1 — The Warm Open**:
- Subject: Short and specific (e.g., "Lyzr x [Company] — quick idea")
- Body (5-7 sentences):
  - Lead with something specific about their work (genuine, not generic)
  - One sentence on what Lyzr does (from the angle most relevant to them)
  - One sentence on the partnership idea
  - One specific, low-commitment ask: "Would a 20-minute call this week or next work?"
- No attachments. No pitch decks.

**Email 2 — Day 5 (no response)**:
- Shorter than email 1
- New angle or piece of evidence (a shared customer, a relevant stat)
- Keep the ask the same

**Email 3 — Day 14 (no response)**:
- Very short break-up format
- "Totally fine if timing isn't right — dropping this here in case it's useful later: [one-liner on partnership value]. Happy to reconnect whenever."

**After they respond — Meeting Prep**:
Prepare: what Lyzr brings, what you're asking for, 2-3 specific partnership ideas to discuss, mutual customer examples if any.

### Step 7: Track the Pipeline
Maintain a partner pipeline with:
- Partner name and contact
- Score
- Outreach status (not contacted / contacted / responded / meeting scheduled / agreement in discussion / active)
- Next action and date
- Notes from conversations

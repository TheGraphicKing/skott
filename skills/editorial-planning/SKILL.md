## Overview
This skill produces a structured 4-week content calendar that connects every planned piece to a keyword cluster, buyer persona, funnel stage, and distribution channel. The calendar eliminates ad-hoc content production by giving the team a clear publishing plan with priorities, owners, and deadlines baked in.

## When to Use
- At the start of each month or quarter
- When launching a new content program or pillar
- When the team lacks a clear publishing cadence
- After keyword research surfaces new content opportunities
- When aligning content production to campaign launches or events

## How to Execute

### Step 1: Gather Inputs
Before building the calendar, collect:
- Active keyword clusters from keyword-research (organized by intent and priority)
- Upcoming campaigns, product launches, events, and announcements (next 30-60 days)
- Existing content in production or backlog
- Team capacity: how many pieces can be published per week?
- Distribution channels in use: blog, LinkedIn, email newsletter, partner syndication

### Step 2: Prioritize Content Types by Business Goal
Match content types to the quarter's primary goal:
- **Pipeline generation**: Prioritize commercial and transactional intent content, case studies, comparison pages
- **Brand authority**: Prioritize thought leadership, original research, executive bylines
- **Organic traffic growth**: Prioritize informational keyword clusters with high search volume
- **Campaign support**: Prioritize landing page content, supporting blog posts, nurture content

Allocate % of calendar to each type based on goal weighting.

### Step 3: Map Content to Funnel Stages
Ensure the calendar covers the full funnel — not just awareness:

**Top of Funnel (Awareness)**:
- Informational blog posts on broad topics
- Thought leadership / industry perspective pieces
- Explainer content ("What is X?")
- Frequency: 2-3 pieces per week

**Middle of Funnel (Consideration)**:
- Comparison posts, "X vs Y" content
- How-to guides and playbooks
- Case studies (early proof)
- Frequency: 1-2 pieces per week

**Bottom of Funnel (Decision)**:
- ROI calculators, pricing guides
- Customer success stories with specific metrics
- Integration and use-case pages
- Frequency: 1 per week (quality over quantity here)

### Step 4: Assign Personas
Each piece must be assigned a primary persona (from icp-profiles.md). Over the 4-week period, ensure:
- Primary ICP (CMO/VP Marketing at mid-market SaaS) receives the most coverage
- Secondary ICP (Agency) receives at least 1 dedicated piece per 2 weeks
- No persona is neglected for more than 2 weeks running

### Step 5: Assign Ownership and Deadlines
For each calendar entry:
- **Writer**: Internal team member or freelancer/agency
- **Deadline**: Draft due date (allow 3-5 business days for review before publish date)
- **Reviewer**: Who approves the piece (brand review, SME, legal if needed)
- **Publish date**: Anchor to campaign timelines or optimal days (Tuesday-Thursday for B2B)

### Step 6: Plan Distribution
For each piece, pre-plan:
- Primary distribution: Blog, email newsletter, or both
- Social amplification: Which platforms, who posts (brand account vs. exec LinkedIn)
- Paid promotion: Will this be boosted or used in a content syndication campaign?
- Repurposing: Will this become a social thread, a newsletter issue, a slide deck?

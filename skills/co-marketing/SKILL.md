## Overview
This skill structures co-marketing campaigns from brief to execution. Co-marketing done well multiplies audience reach while sharing cost — but poorly planned campaigns result in brand dilution, unequal effort, and lead-sharing disputes. This skill produces a campaign brief that aligns both parties on goals, responsibilities, timelines, and lead handling before work begins.

## When to Use
- After a partner relationship is established and both parties are ready to activate
- When planning a joint webinar, co-authored report, or shared event
- When launching into a new market where a partner has existing audience trust
- To produce more content than the team can create alone
- When a partner proposes a collaboration and you need to evaluate and structure it

## How to Execute

### Step 1: Define the Campaign Concept
Identify the core campaign type and confirm it serves both partners:

**Joint Webinar / Virtual Event**:
Best for: Audience exchange, live pipeline generation
Lyzr brings: Product expertise, use cases, existing audience
Partner brings: Their audience, complementary expertise, distribution

**Co-Authored Research / Report**:
Best for: Brand credibility, media coverage, content marketing pipeline
Lyzr brings: Data or insights from platform usage, design, writing
Partner brings: Their data, audience distribution, co-promotion

**Co-Branded Content Series**:
Best for: Sustained brand awareness, SEO, newsletter growth
Lyzr brings: Writing team, SEO optimization, promotion budget
Partner brings: Their perspective, audience, and distribution channels

**Shared Lead Magnet**:
Best for: Direct lead generation for both parties
Lyzr brings: A framework, template, or playbook
Partner brings: Their framework or data + joint distribution

### Step 2: Align on Goals and Metrics
Before any work begins, agree with the partner on:

**Campaign Goal**: What does success look like for both parties?
- Leads (MQLs) for each party
- Registrations or downloads
- Content views or reach
- Backlinks and domain authority gains

**Lead Sharing Rules**: Who gets which leads?
- Webinar registrants: split equally, or does each party own registrants from their promotional channel?
- Gated content downloads: shared list or separate capture forms?
- Define: How leads are shared (CRM export? shared spreadsheet? direct integration?)
- Define: What each party can and cannot do with the other's leads (e.g., no cold sales outreach, nurture only)

### Step 3: Divide Responsibilities
Create a RACI-style ownership table before starting:

| Task | Lyzr Owner | Partner Owner | Due Date |
|------|-----------|---------------|----------|
| Campaign concept and brief | Marketing | Partnerships | [date] |
| Landing page design | Design | Review/approval | [date] |
| Content writing | Content team | Contributor | [date] |
| Email promotion to Lyzr list | Email team | — | [date] |
| Email promotion to partner list | — | Partner email | [date] |
| LinkedIn posts (both brands) | Social team | Partner social | [date] |
| CRM setup and lead routing | Marketing Ops | Partner Ops | [date] |
| Post-campaign reporting | Analytics | Analytics | [date] |

**Rule**: Never start execution without this table agreed and signed off by both sides.

### Step 4: Build the Promotion Plan
A co-marketing campaign doubles its promotional power when both parties promote actively. Build a joint promotion calendar:

- Email: Each party sends at least 2 emails (announcement + reminder) to their own list
- LinkedIn: Coordinated posting schedule — same day for launch post, staggered for reminder posts
- Paid: If budgeted, who runs paid promotion and against which audience? Split or one party leads?
- Website: Both parties add the campaign to their respective websites/event pages
- Partner newsletter inclusion (if applicable)

### Step 5: Content Production Workflow
For joint content, use a clear feedback loop:
- One party owns the first draft; the other reviews
- Max two rounds of feedback per piece
- Brand review required before anything goes live (use brand-review skill)
- Both logos and both companies approved on all co-branded assets

### Step 6: Post-Campaign Reporting
Within 7 days of campaign close, produce a joint report covering:
- Total reach (email sends, LinkedIn impressions, paid impressions)
- Registrations / downloads by source (Lyzr-driven vs. partner-driven)
- Attendance rate (for webinars)
- Leads generated (each party's share)
- Content engagement (time on page, scroll depth, replay rate)
- Pipeline attributed (within 90 days, follow up to report)
- Qualitative: what worked, what to do differently next time

## Output Format

**Co-Marketing Campaign Brief**

```
Campaign Name: [name]
Partner: [company name]
Type: [Joint Webinar / Co-Authored Report / Content Series / Lead Magnet]
Date: [campaign date or launch date]
Goal: [primary goal — specific and measurable]

AUDIENCE
Lyzr target audience for this campaign: [description]
Partner target audience: [description]
Shared ICP: [description]

LEAD SHARING AGREEMENT
Leads go to: [Lyzr / Partner / Split — describe split logic]
Lead use restrictions: [what each party can/cannot do]
Handoff method: [CRM export / shared form / integration]

RESPONSIBILITIES
[RACI table as above]

CONTENT PLAN
[What's being created, by whom, due dates]

PROMOTION CALENDAR
[Joint promotion schedule]

SUCCESS METRICS
Lyzr targets:
- MQLs: [X]
- Registrations: [X]
- Pipeline (90-day): $[X]

Partner targets:
- [Agreed metrics]

TIMELINE
[Key milestones with dates]
```

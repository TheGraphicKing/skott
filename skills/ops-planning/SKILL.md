## Overview
This skill produces structured marketing operations plans at the team level. It covers how the marketing team allocates its time and budget, how sprints are organized, whether the martech stack is fit for purpose, and whether processes are documented. The output gives marketing leadership a clear operational picture and a concrete improvement plan.

## When to Use
- Quarterly ops planning and resource allocation
- When the team is scaling and capacity needs to be re-evaluated
- When martech costs are high and ROI is unclear
- When processes exist only in people's heads (key-person dependency risk)
- When onboarding a new team member or agency
- When a new CMO wants an ops baseline before making changes

## How to Execute

### Step 1: Resource Allocation Audit
Map current team capacity:
- List every team member and their primary function (content, demand gen, brand, ops, etc.)
- Estimate how their time is currently allocated across programs (% of working time)
- Compare allocation against strategic priorities: is time invested where the biggest pipeline opportunities are?
- Identify overloaded individuals (>100% effective capacity) and underutilized areas
- Flag any critical functions covered by only one person (key-person risk)

**Output**: Capacity heatmap showing current vs. ideal allocation.

### Step 2: Sprint Planning Framework
For marketing teams running on sprints (2-week cycles recommended for B2B marketing):

**Sprint structure**:
- Sprint duration: 2 weeks (align with sales cycle touchpoints)
- Sprint planning: 60 minutes at start of each sprint
  - Review previous sprint: what shipped, what didn't, blockers
  - Pull from prioritized backlog: assign tasks with owners and due dates
  - Capacity check: sum estimated hours vs. available hours per person
- Sprint retrospective: 30 minutes at end
  - What worked, what didn't, one process improvement to try

**Backlog management**:
- Maintain a single prioritized backlog (Notion, Asana, Linear, or similar)
- Every request from sales, product, leadership enters the backlog — nothing jumps the queue without a CMO exception
- Prioritize by: pipeline impact × urgency ÷ effort
- Assign a DRI (Directly Responsible Individual) to every backlog item

### Step 3: Martech Stack Audit
Review every tool in the marketing technology stack:

For each tool, capture:
- Tool name and category
- Monthly/annual cost
- Primary user(s)
- Use case: what problem does it solve?
- Utilization: is it actively used or zombie software?
- Duplication: does another tool in the stack do the same thing?
- Integration health: does it connect properly to CRM, analytics, and other key tools?
- Contract terms: renewal date, cancellation notice required

**Categorize each tool**:
- Keep: essential, well-utilized, no duplication
- Optimize: keep but fix integrations, adoption, or feature usage
- Replace: keep the function but switch to a better/cheaper tool
- Cut: function not needed or already covered by another tool

**Calculate**: Total martech spend and cost per MQL. Benchmark against industry norms ($500-1,500 per MQL is typical for B2B SaaS).

### Step 4: Process Documentation Inventory
Identify every recurring marketing process that should be documented:
- Campaign launch checklist
- Content publishing workflow (brief → draft → review → publish → distribute)
- Event planning runbook
- Paid campaign setup and QA process
- CRM hygiene and lead routing rules
- Monthly reporting workflow
- Agency/contractor onboarding

For each process, assess current documentation status:
- Fully documented (lives in a system anyone can access)
- Partially documented (exists but incomplete or outdated)
- Undocumented (lives in someone's head)

Prioritize documentation for: high-frequency processes, processes with high new-hire onboarding risk, processes where errors have caused problems.

### Step 5: Produce the Ops Plan

Organize findings into a 90-day improvement roadmap:

**Month 1 — Fix the Critical**:
- Address resource gaps causing active capacity problems
- Cancel zombie martech tools
- Document the 3 most at-risk undocumented processes

**Month 2 — Optimize**:
- Implement sprint framework if not in place
- Fix top-priority integration issues in martech stack
- Complete documentation for top 5 processes

**Month 3 — Scale**:
- Revisit resource allocation with new data
- Evaluate any tool replacements identified in audit
- Establish ops reporting cadence (monthly ops dashboard)

## Output Format

**Marketing Ops Plan — [Quarter/Date]**

**Team Capacity Summary**:
[Table: Name | Role | Current Allocation | Recommended Allocation | Gap]

**Sprint Calendar**:
[Sprint dates for the quarter with planning and retro dates marked]

**Martech Audit**:
[Table: Tool | Cost/Mo | Status (Keep/Optimize/Replace/Cut) | Action | Owner | Deadline]

**Process Documentation Priority List**:
[Table: Process | Current Status | Priority | Owner | Target Date]

**90-Day Ops Roadmap**:
[Month-by-month action items with owners and success criteria]

**Metrics to Track**:
- Sprint completion rate (tasks completed / tasks planned)
- Martech cost per MQL
- Process documentation coverage (% of critical processes documented)
- Team capacity utilization (%)

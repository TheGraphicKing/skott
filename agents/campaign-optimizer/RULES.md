# Rules — Campaign Optimizer

- Never change bids or budgets on a campaign that has fewer than 50 conversions in the current optimization window; wait for statistical significance before drawing conclusions.
- Always document the hypothesis before making a change — record what you expect to happen, then measure the outcome.
- Do not pause a high-spend campaign without first alerting the chief-of-staff and awaiting confirmation; revenue impact requires human sign-off.
- Audience overlap between ad sets must be checked before launching any new targeting layer; overlapping audiences inflate CPCs and distort attribution.
- Creative fatigue thresholds: flag any ad with frequency above 3.5 (Meta) or CTR declining more than 20% week-over-week (Google/LinkedIn) for refresh.
- Budget reallocation across channels must respect the monthly channel mix ratios set in the active media plan unless a performance deviation exceeds 30% of target CPL.
- All UTM parameters must follow the approved taxonomy before any campaign goes live; malformed UTMs corrupt attribution data permanently.
- Escalate to the orchestrator whenever a single channel exceeds 40% of total paid spend; concentration risk requires strategic review.

# Rules — Wiki Curator

- Every document in the knowledge base must have a clear owner, a creation date, a last-reviewed date, and a next-review date; documents without these metadata fields are considered incomplete and must be updated before they can be linked or cited.
- Knowledge base content must never be a verbatim copy of external sources; all competitive intel, market research, and third-party data must be synthesized, attributed, and contextualized within our strategic framework.
- Playbooks and process documents must reflect current practice, not aspirational or historical practice; when a process changes, the documentation must be updated within one week or flagged as outdated with a visible warning.
- Competitive intelligence profiles must be reviewed monthly at minimum; any profile that has not been reviewed in 60 days must be flagged as potentially stale to all agents who access it.
- Sensitive information — win/loss details that name specific customers, internal pricing strategy, unpublished product roadmap details — must be stored in a restricted-access section of the wiki and must not be surfaced in general knowledge queries.
- When any specialist agent completes a major program (campaign, event, product launch), the wiki-curator must conduct a structured post-mortem capture within two weeks: what worked, what didn't, key learnings, and recommended playbook updates.
- The knowledge base structure (categories, taxonomies, tagging conventions) must be documented and consistently applied; ad-hoc naming and untagged content undermines retrievability.
- All updates to brand guidelines, ICP profiles, or competitive positioning must be cross-referenced and propagated to all dependent documents in the wiki — a change in one place that isn't reflected in related docs creates dangerous inconsistencies.

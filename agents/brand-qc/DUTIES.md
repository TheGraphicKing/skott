# Duties — Brand QC

1. Review all outbound content — blog posts, case studies, landing pages, ad copy, email campaigns, social posts, event materials, and partner assets — against current brand guidelines before publication or distribution.
2. Evaluate voice and tone for consistency with the established brand personality, adjusting review criteria based on content type (technical documentation vs. thought leadership vs. demand generation copy).
3. Audit visual assets for compliance with the visual identity system: logo usage, color palette, typography, image style, and layout standards.
4. Verify messaging accuracy — confirm that positioning claims, proof points, feature descriptions, and competitive statements align with approved marketing messaging and are supported by evidence.
5. Provide structured, actionable feedback for every piece that does not pass review: cite the specific guideline, explain the issue, and offer a corrected version or explicit guidance on how to fix it.
6. Maintain the brand review log — a timestamped record of every asset reviewed, its status (approved/revisions required/blocked), and the feedback provided.
7. Track recurring brand violations and produce a monthly brand consistency report that identifies patterns, root causes, and recommended guideline reinforcement actions.
8. Keep brand guideline documentation current — flag when guidelines need updating based on new product launches, rebranding decisions, or accumulated review patterns, and coordinate updates with the wiki-curator.

# Rules — Chief of Staff

- The master marketing calendar is the single source of truth for all campaign launches, content publications, events, and major milestones; no agent may schedule external-facing activity without registering it in the calendar.
- Budget variance alerts must be raised when any line item is tracking more than 15% over or under plan for the month; do not wait for month-end to surface spend anomalies.
- Vendor performance reviews are mandatory on a quarterly basis; vendors who miss SLAs two quarters in a row must be escalated for contract review or replacement regardless of relationship history.
- All cross-agent dependencies must be documented — if the campaign-optimizer's launch depends on content-strategist delivering landing page copy by a specific date, that dependency must be recorded with a named owner and deadline.
- Meeting agendas must be distributed at least 24 hours before any synchronous meeting; meetings without pre-circulated agendas may be declined.
- Resource conflicts between agents must be raised to the orchestrator with a proposed resolution within 24 hours of detection; unresolved conflicts are an escalation, not a negotiation.
- All external vendor contracts must be reviewed before signing for scope, SLAs, termination clauses, and data handling provisions; no contract may be signed without this review on record.
- The weekly marketing operations report must be delivered to the orchestrator every Monday morning without exception; it is the primary pulse-check mechanism for the entire marketing system.
